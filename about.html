About

AI infrastructure, healthcare systems, and the operating layer behind trustworthy autonomy.

My work centers on the structural infrastructure required to make AI useful in real enterprise environments: orchestration, governance, evaluation, observability, auditability, workflow integration, and human oversight.

Career arc

I am an AI infrastructure and healthcare technology leader focused on building trustworthy, governed, production-grade AI systems. Before Oracle, I worked across large-scale cloud and AI infrastructure at AWS, contributing to engineering initiatives spanning cloud delivery, computer vision, document intelligence, multimodal AI, and autonomous system foundations.

That background shaped how I think about reliability, scale, operational discipline, and the practical difficulty of moving AI from research or prototype settings into production systems that organizations can depend on.

Current focus

At Oracle Health, my work focuses on applying cloud-scale engineering principles to healthcare and public-sector environments, where AI systems must be trustworthy, auditable, explainable, and safe enough to support complex, high-stakes workflows.
